A repeatable hosting procedure

The goal: every service gets a hostname, HTTPS, auth (if needed), logs, and a backup plan.
This keeps “random port sprawl” under control.

1) Pick a naming scheme
Examples: app.lab.local, grafana.lab, jellyfin.home
2) Put a reverse proxy in front
One “front door” on 443 routes traffic to internal services
3) Add TLS
Certificates + auto-renew = fewer surprises
4) Require auth where appropriate
SSO (OIDC) or basic auth for admin panels
5) Observe + backup
Metrics/logs + backups you've actually restored once
Try not to expose anything publicly unless you have to. Prefer VPN access (WireGuard/Tailscale/OpenVPN) over port-forwarding.
When you must port-forward, keep it minimal and monitor it.

Reverse proxy patterns

  • Path-based routing: https://home/plex → one domain, multiple apps
  • Host-based routing: https://plex.home → cleanest for most labs
  • TLS termination: proxy handles certs, apps stay internal

Operational checklists

  • Backups: define RPO/RTO, automate, test restores quarterly.
  • Updates: pick a window; keep a rollback plan (snapshots help).
  • Secrets: store in a vault or at least env files with tight permissions.
  • Monitoring: uptime checks + basic CPU/RAM/disk metrics + logs.
  • Docs: one page per service: purpose, URL, dependencies, backup path.
